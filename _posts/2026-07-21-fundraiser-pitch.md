## Fundraiser Pitch
My fundraiser team, Tailored for Tails, had been preparing for a month to present the perfect pitch. After all, only one team's pitch out of 33 groups would be picked for the fundraiser. Though we were a week behind compared to the other teams, everyone dedicated their time to the project and worked hard to catch up—the day before the pitch, I even found my teammates working on the speech at 3 A.M.
### Our Idea
Our group consisted of four people who each had an official role: CEO, Logistics, PR, and Marketing. As the CEO, I ensured everyone was focused, organized, and on the same page. The week leading up to the pitch kept the team busy with work as we still needed to make several edits to the pitch and decide on the final changes to our potential fundraiser idea. 

If our fundraiser got picked, we would donate the money earned to an organization called KARA (Korea Animal Rights Advocates) since we wanted to focus on animal rescue and abandoned animals. The basics of our pitch idea were to have students line up and enter the "scanning room" to have their faces scanned. This "room" would catch the attention of students and seem fun at first glance. Additionally, once they entered the room, we would scan their faces with our eyes through a camera set up on the "scanner", which would actually be a computer that presents slides with scanning videos. (Though using artificial intelligence may seem to be more convenient for this, we declined the suggestion mostly due to the risk of unintentionally violating privacy and portrait rights.) The video on the computer also allows the students to experience the simulation of getting scanned. 

The student in the scanning room would exit and go around the room to be met by a booth, which would be run by two of our teammates. One would check the faces of the students in the scanning room through a device connected to the camera and pick out a look-alike animal of that student. She would then pass a small board with an image of the chosen animal to the teammate near the front of the booth. This teammate would then explain to the student who had just come out of the scanning room what their look-alike animal is. The twist is that all these animals would be ones that had been rescued, and we would show before and after pictures of these animals. The teammate would then ask the student to donate to KARA after a brief explanation of the organization and animal abandonment in South Korea.

### Pitch Day
The pitch went fairly well. We presented in front of several students and teachers. The audience asked us some questions, and even though most of the questions that were asked were not the ones we had prepared for, I was quite surprised by our ability to answer questions on the spot and without hesitation.

A few weeks later, my team received a bronze medal for our pitch. Though our idea was not picked, I am still proud of my team and grateful for the time they dedicated to the project despite their busy schedules.

### Actual Fundraiser Preparation
Currently, I am working on the actual 'market', which has been decided to be a fundraiser aiming to earn 2 million won with the intent of donating all the money to Seoul Green Trust. I have taken on the role as part of the PR department. As of right now, I am finalizing the door-to-door sales script for next week and making sure that all necessary information is communicated between all the departments.
