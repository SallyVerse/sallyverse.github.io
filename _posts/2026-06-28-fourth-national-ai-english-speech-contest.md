## Unintentionally summoning all my negative nerves 🙂
A week before the competition, a notification on my phone went off, politely informing me that I  had passed the Preliminary Round and advanced to the Finals. I didn't think I would become a finalist, considering my lack of experience, but, luckily, I did! I'm sure many people would have jumped up and down with delight if they had been in my shoes.
But me?
I knew that this was great news, and although I was definitely excited to have gotten into the Finals, I had several worries.
In fact, here are some of the thoughts that entered my brain—against my will—after I saw that message:
*What if I fall off the stage, smack my face straight into the ground, lose a few of my teeth, and knock into a bunch of expensive equipment that I would end up having to pay for?*
*What if I suddenly lose the ability to speak English and gravely embarrass myself in front of my peers and their parents, bringing shame to my family name?*
*And remember what happened in…third grade?*
I understand that these are extremely unrealistic events that would most likely not occur unless I have the worst luck in human history, *but* keep in mind that this was my very first speech contest, so my anxiety was reasonable.
However, a few days before the competition, I began to gain some confidence, and soon enough, most of my nerves had subsided. Over the span of three days, several deep breaths and reminders to go into that competition with determination did the trick.
## Competition Day
Luckily, on the day of the competition, my confidence had *not* abandoned me, which was great news since without it, I probably would have found my way back to the illogical scenarios from before.
I even recall feeling a sort of giddiness before the contest began.
### Preparation
Once all 20 students were seated (10 each for the Middle School Division and High School Division) and the announcements were over, the competition officially commenced, leaving us with two hours on the clock to incorporate AI in our speeches and to create an additional video or image to demonstrate how well we could actually use artificial intelligence.
I barely looked up from my laptop, but from the silence in the room I could tell that everyone was just as focused and determined as I was.
After our two hours of preparation, we had lunchtime, but I spent most of that period practicing my speech.
### It's Time
The second competitor had finished her speech, followed by a round of applause.
I knew that all the competitors would be very strong contenders, but *wow*. I know only two students had gone up, but they did amazing.
My eyes followed the announcer's hand as it dived straight into the box of names. Call me crazy, but I just knew I would get picked.
As I had predicted, the announcer called my name, and I rose up from my seat, walking up towards the podium.
One foot after another, I came closer and closer to the stage.
I guess this was my time to shine.
### What Happened?
Thank goodness.
Aside from a small technical issue, the presentation went smoothly. I did stutter a few times, but I'm rather satisfied with how I did.
If you were wondering, I did *not* fall off the stage, lose my teeth, or disgrace my family.
I spent so much time anticipating the worst case scenarios when I could have just relaxed and looked forward to the competition day. The contest led me to realize that sometimes the best way to do things is to have fun instead of obsessing over what could go wrong.
