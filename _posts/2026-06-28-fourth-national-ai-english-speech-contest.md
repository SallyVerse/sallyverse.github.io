# Unintentionally summoning all the negative nerves in my possession 🙂

A week before the competition, a notification on my phone went off, politely informing me that I  had passed the Preliminary Round and advanced to the Finals. A normal student probably would have reacted by spending the rest of their day jumping up and down with a seemingly permanent smile on their face.

But me?

I am not a normal student.

In fact, here are some of the thoughts that entered my brain (against my will) after I saw that message:

*What if I fall off the stage, smack my face straight into the ground, lose a few of my teeth, and knock into a bunch of expensive equipment that I would end up having to pay for?*

*What if I suddenly lose the ability to speak English and gravely embarrass myself in front of my peers and their parents, bringing shame to my family name?*

*And remember what happened in…third grade?*

I understand that these are extremely unrealistic events that would most likely never occur unless I have the worst luck in human history, *but* keep in mind that this was my very first speech contest, so my anxiety was very reasonable—not to mention that the title of the contest sounded much too fancy and official.

However, a few days before the competition, I began to gain some confidence, and soon enough, most of my nerves had subsided. My nervous thoughts were replaced by a noticeably inexperienced evil laugh that spawned in my head, echoing against the walls of my mind—and, yes, I do know that's not how brains work—until I reminded myself that I am a mature, sophisticated teenager who is fully capable of acting seriously and professionally, as I should.

# D-Day

Luckily, on the day of the competition, my confidence had *not* abandoned me, which was great news since without it, I probably would have found my way back to the illogical scenarios from before—all thanks to my concerningly wild imagination.

I even recall feeling a sort of giddiness before the contest began.

## Preparation

Once all 20 students were seated (10 each for the Middle School Division and High School Division) and the announcements were over, the competition officially commenced, leaving us with two hours on the clock to incorporate AI in our speeches and to create an additional video or image to demonstrate how well we could actually use artificial intelligence.

I barely looked up from my laptop, but from the silence in the room I could tell that everyone was just as focused and determined as I was.
After our two hours of preparation, we had lunchtime, but I spent most of that period practicing my speech.

## It's Time

The second competitor had finished her speech, followed by a round of applause.

My eyes followed the announcer's hand as it dived straight into the box of names. Call me crazy, but I just knew I would get picked.

As I had predicted, the announcer called my name, and I rose up from my seat, walking up towards the podium.

One foot after another, I came closer and closer to the stage.
I guess this was my time to shine.

## What Happened?

Thank goodness.

Aside from a small technical issue, the presentation went smoothly. I didn't stumble across my words too much. I'm rather satisfied with how I did.

Though I didn't earn first place, this contest served to be a great experience and much encouragement to do better for the next public speaking competition.
